Vegan Lemon Loaf Cake

Apr 5, 2015 • Shanna Patel

After a week in New Jersey, I’m back in England. And unlike New Jersey which saw snow just a few days ago, there are daffodils and blossom everywhere in London. This recipe is lovely for springtime; it is light, and full of zesty lemon.

This recipe is vegan, however you could substitute the nut milk for dairy milk if you prefer.

I decorated mine with caramelised lemon slices. If you’d like to do the same, simply slice a lemon finely with a mandolin, make a simple caramel and caramelise the lemon slices on each side for 15-20 seconds. Let them cool before topping the loaf cake.

Happy Baking!

Ingredients

For the loaf cake: 1 3/4 cup self-raising flour 1/2 cup caster sugar or extra-fine granulated sugar 1/4 tsp salt 1 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/2 tsp bicarbonate of soda / baking soda 1/4 cup desiccated coconut 3 tbsp brown rice syrup or golden syrup 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar 3/4 cup + 2 tbsp unsweetened almond milk 1/2 cup sunflower oil For the lemon glaze: 1/2 cup icing/powdered sugar Zest and juice of 1 small lemon or 1/2 a large lemon

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 350F or 180C.
  2. Line your loaf tin with baking parchment.
  3. In a mixing bowl or the bowl of your stand mixer, sift the first 5 ingredients in the above order (flour to baking powder).
  4. Add the coconut and syrup.
  5. Add the vinegar, which will react with the bicarbonate of soda.
  6. Swiftly add the almond milk and oil.
  7. Mix the ingredients together until you have a smooth cake batter. If you feel it is too thick, you may add up to 2 more tbsp of milk.
  8. Pour the cake batter into the lined tin. Ensure it is spread evenly.
  9. Bake for 25 minutes.
  10. Tear a piece of foil large enough to cover the tin. Quickly and gently remove the cake from the oven and cover with the foil.
  11. Return to oven for a further 20 minutes.
  12. To be sure the cake has baked all the way through, pierce with a skewer. If it comes out clean, it's ready.
  13. Allow the cake to cool totally before icing.
  14. Make the icing by sifting the icing/powdered sugar and adding the juice and zest. Mix until smooth.
  15. Remove the cake from the tin.
  16. Using a piping bag* ice the cake as you wish. I like to just zigzag across repeatedly. *You could also just fill a ziploc bag with the icing, snip a tiny part of the corner off, and use that.
