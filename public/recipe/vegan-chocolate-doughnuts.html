Vegan Chocolate Doughnuts

Jun 15, 2015 • Shanna Patel

These doughnuts are surprisingly simple to make. I’m not a fan of deep frying, but these really are worth the effort/calories! If you aren’t vegan, you can substitute the vegan cream for heavy whipping cream and the vegan shortening for butter.

Ingredients

Yield: 12 small doughnuts 2 tbsp granulated sugar 3/4 tsp active dry yeast 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/4 tsp sea salt 1/4 cup dairy-free cream 1 tbsp vegan shortening/spread 1/2 cup vegan bittersweet chocolate chips 1/2 cup powdered sugar 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon 2 cups neutral oil for frying, such as sunflower or canola

Method

  1. Combine the yeast and sugar with 1/2 cup of warm water and leave to one side for 10 minutes, until the yeast has begun to foam.
  2. While you wait, measure out the flour, salt and baking powder in a mixing bowl and stir to combine.
  3. In a small saucepan on a low heat, warm the vegan cream and spread until the spread has just melted, then remove from the heat.
  4. Add the cream mixture to the flour mixture and stir until just combined.
  5. Add the yeast mixture to bowl and combine until a dough begins to form.
  6. Knead the dough for a couple of minutes until smooth.
  7. Place the dough in a greased bowl. Cover with cling film and leave in a warm place to rise for 45 minutes.
  8. Once the dough has risen, divide it into 12 equal sized dough balls.
  9. To make a doughnut, flatten one dough ball in the palm of your hand, add approximately 8 chocolate chips to the centre of the ball, then pinch the edges together to seal. Continue for the remaining 11.
  10. Heat your fryer on a low to medium heat. You don't want the oil to smoke, so keep an eye on it.
  11. While it heats, prepare your powdered sugar by sifting it and mixing in the cinnamon powder. Prepare a plate covered with absorbent kitchen paper for the fried doughnuts.
  12. To test if your oil is at the right temperature for frying, place a tiny piece of dough in the oil if it bubbles and stays at the surface, it's ready.
  13. Once your oil is hot enough, fry your doughnuts, 3 at a time. Let them cook for at least 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown. The outside colour of the doughnut can be deceiving - if they don't cook long enough, you'll be left with raw dough inside.
  14. Once cooked, lay them on the kitchen paper for a minute.
  15. Toss the doughnuts in the powdered sugar and cinnamon and serve immediately
