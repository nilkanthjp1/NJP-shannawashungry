Individual Brownie Cheesecakes (Ekadashi)

Feb 25, 2015 • Shanna Patel

Guys! I’ve never been good at maths but the following formula is 100% correct:
Grain-free brownies + egg-free brownies – refined sugar = PERFECT GUILT FREE BROWNIES (that can also be made for Ekadashi).

Yup, that’s right. Honestly they’re super scrummy without the cheesecake topping. But I’m not gonna be that girl who said cheesecake is unnecessary. So make them with the cheesecake topping, ok?

A few things to mention:

  • I used silicone moulds like these for this recipe and would recommend you do the same. If you don’t have silicone moulds, a mini muffin tray lined with muffin cases would work just as well.
  • When I say individual, I really do mean tiny – maybe 1 square inch. If you want bigger morsels, simply double/treble the recipe and use a cupcake tray.
  • Buckwheat is absolutely not a grain, and can be consumed on Ekadashi.
  • There are only two brownies in the photos because the others were gobbled quite early on. No regrets and no apologies.

Ingredients

Yield: makes 12 bite-size cheesecakes For the base: 1 tbsp and 1 tsp coconut oil, melted 1/4 cup maple syrup 1 tsp baking powder 1/2 tsp of salt 1/4 cup ground almonds 4 tsp buckwheat flour 1/4 cup cocoa powder 1 tbsp and 1 tsp almond milk For the cheesecake: 100g cream cheese 28ml sour cream 1 tbsp sugar 1 tbsp + 1 tsp Ener-G egg replacer (a combination of potato and tapioca starch) 2 tbsp almond milk

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Prepare your brownie moulds - silicone baking moulds are ideal because the brownies can be removed more easily. If you don't have them, you could use cupcake cases in a cupcake tray.
  3. In a bowl combine the melted coconut oil and maple syrup.
  4. In succession, add the salt, baking powder, flour and cocoa.
  5. Fold in the ground almonds.
  6. Add in the almond milk and mix until you have a smooth brownie mix.
  7. Divide the brownie mixture between 12 cases or moulds.
  8. Bake for 6 minutes.
  9. Remove from oven and allow to cool.
  10. Combine all the cheesecake ingredients together.
  11. Top the brownies with the cheesecake mixture - between 1 1/2 and 2 tsp per brownie - and spread smoothly and as flat as possible.
  12. Return your moulds to the oven for 10 minutes.
  13. Switch off your oven BUT leave the cheesecakes in there for a further 5 minutes.
  14. Remove from oven and allow to cool to room temperature.
  15. Refrigerate the cheesecakes for at least 2 hours. (If using silicone moulds, use the freezer for 45 minutes. They'll pop out of the moulds easily)
  16. Remove the cheesecake from the moulds and serve.
