Triple Chocolate Muffins

Mar 16, 2015 • Shanna Patel

These muffins will always satisfy one of those irrational need-chocolate-NOW cravings. Loaded with bitter cocoa, white chocolate and 70% cocoa dark chocolate, they’re rich and comforting.
This is also a super quick, one bowl recipe so you should be in and out of the kitchen in 30 minutes!

 

Ingredients

Yield: 6 large muffins 3/4 cup self raising flour 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa 1/4 cup caster sugar/ extra-fine granulated sugar 1/4 tsp baking soda (bicarbonate of soda) 3/4 tsp baking powder a pinch of salt 1 1/2 tbsp golden syrup (if you don't have golden syrup, you could substitute honey) 6 tbsp warm milk 1/4 cup sunflower oil or other neutral oil 1/2 tbsp vinegar White and dark chocolate, 25g of each

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 180C or 350F.
  2. Line a 6 cup muffin tray with paper cases.
  3. Chop your white and dark chocolate into chunks.
  4. In a mixing bowl, sift the dry ingredients in the following order: cocoa, sugar, flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder.
  5. Add the golden syrup but avoid placing it on the baking powder and baking soda.
  6. Add the vinegar directly onto the baking powder and soda. The ingredients will react and you’ll see lots of bubbles. This is exactly what you’re looking for.
  7. In quick succession, add the milk and oil.
  8. Mix the ingredients together until smooth. Beat for a further two minutes.
  9. Spoon the muffin mixture into the cases, distributing it evenly between the 6 - about 2 generous tablespoons per muffin.
  10. Top each muffin with the white and dark chocolate chunks.
  11. Bake the muffins for 16 minutes. When the top of the muffin is pressed gently, it should bounce back up. If it doesn't cook for a further 2 minutes, until it does bounce back.
