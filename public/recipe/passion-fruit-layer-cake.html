Passion Fruit Layer Cake

Mar 22, 2015 • Shanna Patel

I made this cake recently for my cousin’s birthday. It was the first time I combined these two flavours and they are a match made in heaven! The coconut sponge sandwiches a vanilla bean mascarpone and passion fruit. The sharp tang of the fruit is perfect with the creamy vanilla mascarpone.

This cake is great for a special occasion — unusual flavours, simple to make and very eye-catching.

You will need two 8 inch cake tins for this recipe.

Ingredients

For the coconut sponge: 1 3/4 cup self-raising flour 1/2 cup caster sugar or extra-fine granulated sugar 1 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/2 tsp bicarbonate of soda / baking soda 1/4 tsp salt 3 tbsp golden syrup 3/4 + 2 tbsp warm milk 1 tbsp vinegar 1/2 cup sunflower oil 1/4 cup desiccated coconut For the filling: 4 passion fruits 150g mascarpone 150g fromage frais* 1 tbsp caster sugar / extra-fine granulated sugar 1/2 tsp vanilla bean paste *I haven't found fromage frais in US supermarkets, although it may well be available. I would suggest substituting low fat cream cheese or non-fat greek yogurt if you can't find it.

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 180C or 350F.
  2. Line your two 8 inch cake tins with baking parchment.
  3. In a mixing bowl, sift the dry ingredients in the following order: sugar, flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder, coconut
  4. Add the golden syrup but avoid placing it on the baking powder and baking soda. If you haven't already, warm the milk.
  5. Add the vinegar directly onto the baking powder and soda. The ingredients will react and you’ll see lots of bubbles. This is exactly what you’re looking for.
  6. In quick succession, add the milk and oil.
  7. Mix the ingredients together until smooth. Beat for a further two minutes.
  8. Divide the cake batter evenly between the two cake tins.
  9. Ensure that the cake batter is evenly spread in the tins, so that they bake evenly.
  10. Bake for 20 minutes, until the sponges are golden. If you press the top of the sponge gently it ought to bounce back up. Alternatively, pierce with a skewer. If it comes out clean, the sponge is cooked all the way through.
  11. Let the sponges sit for 10 minutes before removing them from their tins. This will accelerate the cooling time. They must be at room temperature when you assemble the cake. If you aren't in a hurry, let them cool in the baking tins for 2-3 hours.
  12. When you're ready to assemble, combine the mascarpone, sugar, fromage frais and vanilla bean paste.
  13. Halve one passion fruit and add it's flesh to the mascarpone mixture.
  14. Halve the rest of the passion fruits and empty out the flesh and juice into a small bowl. Decide which sponge cake will be your bottom layer. Spoon approximately 5 tsp of the passionfruit juice onto the top of your bottom layer.
  15. Spoon the mascarpone mixture onto the centre of your cake, leaving the outer 1.5 inches untouched - the mixture will spread outwards when you place the other sponge on top.
  16. Now add the remaining passion fruit flesh onto the mascarpone mixture. Let this come to the edge of the cake, it looks so beautiful.
  17. Place the top layer of the cake carefully so that they are in line with one another.
  18. Refrigerate your cake. Remove from the fridge 10 minutes before serving.
  19. Dust with icing sugar just before serving.
