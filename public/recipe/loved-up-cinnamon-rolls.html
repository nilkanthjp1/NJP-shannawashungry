Loved Up Cinnamon Rolls

Feb 12, 2015 • Shanna Patel

Cinnamon rolls (mmm). Little cinnamon rolls (aww). Little heart-shaped cinnamon rolls with a maple glaze (yeahhs).

These rolls are simple to make and with a slight variation in rolling technique, the classic spirals of dough transform into adorable hearts. They add some novelty and are rather yummy if I do say so myself.

May I also suggest that one day you replace the cinnamon filling with Nutella? You will not regret it!

Bonne Baking! xo

Yield: makes 20 miniature rolls

Ingredients

For the dough: 1 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1 1/2 tbsp granulated sugar 1/2 tsp salt 1 1/4 tsp active dry yeast 1/4 cup water 1/2 cup milk 1 tbsp + 1 tsp unsalted butter For the filling: 1 tbsp + 1 tsp unsalted butter, softened to room temperature (it needs to be spreadable) 1.5 tsp ground cinnamon ¼ cup brown sugar For the glaze: 2 tbsp powdered sugar 1/2 tsp maple syrup 1 1/4 tsp milk

Method

  1. Measure out the dry ingredients into a bowl.
  2. Heat your milk and water to approximately 115F. The warmth is necessary to activate the yeast. Avoid scalding water, is this will kill the yeast.
  3. Stir the milk, water and melted butter into the flour mixture.
  4. Combine until you have a soft dough.
  5. It should have an elastic feel to it and come away clean from the sides of the bowl.
  6. Knead your dough for 3 minutes on a floured surface.
  7. Divide the dough into 2 balls.
  8. Place the dough balls in a bowl, cover and leave to one side for 10 minutes.
  9. In the mean time, make your filling. Combine the cinnamon and sugar in a bowl.
  10. Line 2 baking trays with parchment.
  11. After ten minutes, roll out one dough ball on a floured surface to a rectangle approximately 9x11 inches.
  12. Warm your coconut oil if it’s solid, and spread half of it onto the dough.
  13. Sprinkle half of the sugar and cinnamon mixture evenly across the dough.
  14. Using a pizza slicer cut the edges of your dough so that you're left with an even rectangle. This will ensure your hearts are even.
  15. Roll the longer sides of the rectangle towards each other carefully.
  16. Stop when both sides meet in the middle.
  17. Dab a drop of water in between where the two coils meet and press the sides together gently.
  18. Cut the dough into ten evenly sized rolls, using the pizza slicer.
  19. To make the heart shape, simply pinch the bottom of the roll into a point.
  20. Repeat steps 11-19 with the second dough ball.
  21. Place the 20 hearts on the 2 baking trays, leaving 3 inches between each one. Cover loosely with foil or cling film and leave the rolls to rest for at least 60 minutes in a warm place. They should swell slightly.
  22. Preheat your oven to 356F or 180C.
  23. Bake for 15-17 minutes, until the undersides are golden brown.
  24. Remove from the oven and let the rolls cool on the tray.
  25. Make the glaze by combining the three ingredients.
  26. Once your rolls have cooled, drizzle the glaze over each one.
  27. Devour.
