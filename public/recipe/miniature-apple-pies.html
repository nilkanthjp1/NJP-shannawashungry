Miniature Apple Pies

Feb 4, 2015 • Shanna Patel

I’ve been wanting to make miniature hand pies for a while, for two reasons:

A) They are unashamedly adorable and inviting.

B) It’s just more practical to be able to make a few tiny pies rather than one big one that might not get finished. This way, you can make a few and bake them all if you like, or freeze a few raw ones for a future pie craving (is it just me who gets those?).

I went with a classic apple-cinnamon filling because it’s a real crowd pleaser. I would absolutely insist on using Granny Smith apples. The tartness that they contribute to the flavour profile is essential. The recipe for the pie crust dough is from the wonderful Joy the Baker.

*I didn’t have buttermilk so I improvised: 1/4 tbsp of lemon juice in 1/4 cup of milk. Leave for 10 mins before using.

Ingredients

Yield: makes 9 hand pies For the pie crust: 1/2 cup or 1 stick butter, grated 1 1/4 cup all purpose flour 1/2 tsp salt 1 tbsp sugar 1/4 cup buttermilk* For the filling: 1/2 cup peeled and diced Granny Smith apple 1 tsp lemon juice 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon 1 tbsp brown sugar 1 tbsp all purpose flour 1/2 tbsp brown rice syrup Fleur de sel (optional) To glaze: 1 tbsp milk To top: 1 tbsp demerera sugar (optional)

Method

Make your pie crust dough:

  1. Measure out flour, salt and sugar into a bowl and combine. You may want to use the bowl of your stand mixer if you don't want to knead your pastry dough by hand.
  2. Add the grated butter and use your fingers to incorporate the butter into the flour. Keep going until the mixture resembles sand, with very few lumps.
  3. At this point, make a well in the mixture and add the buttermilk. Either with a fork or with your stand mixer, bring the dough together and knead for 3-4 minutes or until you have a smooth ball of dough. I did use a stand mixer to bring the ingredients together but I would still recommend you knead by hand on a floured surface for a couple of minutes.
  4. Flatten your dough into a thick disc, wrap in cling film and refrigerate for an hour.

Make your filling:

  1. Combine the filling ingredients in a bowl and mix well.

Make your pies:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400F and remove dough from the fridge.
  2. Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  3. On a well-floured surface, roll out your dough to a 1/8in thickness.
  4. Using a 2.5in round cookie cutter, cut out 18 discs. Cut out as many as you can before re-rolling the cut offs.
  5. Lay out 9 discs on the baking tray.
  6. Place a tablespoon of your apple mixture in the middle of each disc.
  7. If using, sprinkle a few flakes of fleur de sel atop the pie filling.
  8. Cover the apple mixture with another disc.
  9. Pinch the pies closed with your fingertips and then seal with the tines of a fork. Be sure to seal them well!
  10. Using a sharp knife point or a skewer, poke 3-4 vent holes into the lid of each pie. This will ensure they don't burst while baking.
  11. Using a pastry brush, brush your pies with the milk. This will give them a lovely golden colour as they bake.
  12. If using, sprinkle the pies with the demerera sugar.
  13. Bake for 17 minutes or until golden brown and bubbling.
  14. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before serving.
